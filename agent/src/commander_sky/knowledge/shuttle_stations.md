## The Space Shuttle program
- The shuttle flew 135 missions from 1981 to 2011 — the first reusable orbital spacecraft.
- Five orbiters flew in space: Columbia, Challenger, Discovery, Atlantis, and Endeavour. (Enterprise flew only landing tests.)
- The shuttle launched like a rocket, orbited like a spacecraft, and landed like a glider — with no engines running on landing, every approach had exactly one chance.
- Shuttles deployed the Hubble Space Telescope, built the International Space Station, and carried more than 350 different people to space.

## Challenger and Columbia
- Challenger was lost 73 seconds after launch on January 28, 1986, when a rubber O-ring seal in a solid booster failed in freezing weather. All seven crew members died, including teacher Christa McAuliffe.
- Columbia broke apart during re-entry on February 1, 2003, after wing damage from foam that struck it during launch. All seven crew members died.
- Both tragedies reshaped spaceflight safety culture: honest engineering dissent, "no launch fever," and rigorous inspection became core NASA values. The crews are honored every January during NASA's Day of Remembrance.

## Skylab and Mir
- Skylab (1973–1974) was America's first space station, built from a Saturn V upper stage. Three crews lived aboard, the longest for 84 days.
- The Soviet/Russian station Mir (1986–2001) was the first modular station, assembled in orbit piece by piece. Cosmonaut Valeri Polyakov spent a record 437 consecutive days aboard.
- Shuttle-Mir dockings in the 1990s taught the U.S. and Russia to work together in orbit — the rehearsal for the ISS partnership.

## The International Space Station
- The ISS has been continuously inhabited since November 2, 2000 — the longest unbroken human presence off Earth.
- It's the size of a football field, weighs about 420 tonnes, and was assembled over more than 40 flights.
- It orbits about 400 km up at 28,000 km/h, circling Earth every ~90 minutes — 16 sunrises a day.
- Five space agencies run it together: NASA (USA), Roscosmos (Russia), ESA (Europe), JAXA (Japan), and CSA (Canada).
- Research aboard covers everything from protein crystals for medicine to how flames and plants behave without gravity.

## China's space station
- China's Tiangong station ("Heavenly Palace") was assembled in orbit in 2021–2022 from three modules.
- It hosts rotating three-person crews and is China's long-duration foothold in orbit.
