## Sputnik and the start of the Space Age
- October 4, 1957: the Soviet Union launched Sputnik 1, the first artificial satellite — a polished metal sphere 58 cm across that beeped on radio for three weeks.
- A month later, Sputnik 2 carried the dog Laika, the first animal to orbit Earth.
- The launches triggered the "Space Race" and led to the creation of NASA in 1958.

## Vostok and Yuri Gagarin
- April 12, 1961: Soviet cosmonaut Yuri Gagarin became the first human in space, completing one orbit in Vostok 1 in 108 minutes.
- His callsign was "Kedr" (cedar), and he famously said "Poyekhali!" — "Let's go!" — at liftoff.
- Valentina Tereshkova became the first woman in space aboard Vostok 6 in June 1963, orbiting Earth 48 times.

## Project Mercury
- America's first human spaceflight program (1958–1963) flew six astronauts of the "Mercury Seven."
- Alan Shepard's 15-minute suborbital flight on May 5, 1961 made him the first American in space.
- John Glenn became the first American to orbit Earth on February 20, 1962, aboard Friendship 7. Decades later, in 1998, Glenn flew again on the space shuttle at age 77 — the oldest person in space at that time.
- Mathematician Katherine Johnson hand-verified the orbital calculations for Glenn's flight at his personal request.

## Project Gemini
- Ten crewed flights in 1965–1966 taught NASA everything Apollo would need: spacewalks, rendezvous, docking, and long-duration flight.
- Ed White made America's first spacewalk on Gemini 4 (June 1965) and enjoyed it so much he called coming back inside "the saddest moment of my life."
- Gemini 8 (Neil Armstrong and David Scott, 1966) performed the first docking in space — then survived a violent thruster failure that spun the capsule nearly once per second. Armstrong's calm recovery helped earn him the Apollo 11 command.

## The first spacewalk
- Soviet cosmonaut Alexei Leonov made the first spacewalk on March 18, 1965, floating outside for about 12 minutes.
- His suit ballooned so much in vacuum that he had to bleed air out of it to fit back through the airlock — a secret for decades.
